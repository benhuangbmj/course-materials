\begin{frame}
\textbf{Problem:} Suppose the length of an ordered sequence is $n$. Explain how you will {\color{red}estimate the number of iterations} it takes to find a number with the binary search to find a particular number in the sequence, even for the worst case.\pause

In the binary search, at least a half of the sequence will be dropped. \pause 
In the worst case, \pause 
we will have to cut the sequence down to one element. \pause
Mathematically, that means, we have to keep dividing $n$ by 2 until it's not greater than 1. \pause
Suppose it takes $m$ iterations to cut the sequence down to 1 element, then \pause
\begin{align*}
\onslide<7->{
\frac{n}{\substack{\underbrace{2\cdot2 \cdots 2}\\ m\text{ copies}}} &= 1\\} 
\onslide<8->
{\frac{n}{2^m} = 1\\}
\onslide<9->
{n = 2^m}
\end{align*}
\onslide<10->{
As a consequence, $m = \onslide<11->{\log_2n}$.
}
\end{frame}

## Linear Equations and Systems in the Regents Algebra 1 Exam

Linear equations and systems of linear equations are fundamental to the Regents Algebra 1 exam. They appear frequently, sometimes as direct questions and often as the key step within word problems, graphs, tables, and real-world contexts. These topics are highly procedural, meaning that with practice, the steps become routine and reliable. Because so many problems depend on setting up and solving linear equations correctly, strong skills here lead to consistent points across the exam. A solid understanding of linear equations and systems also provides the foundation for solving linear inequalities. Together, these topics account for roughly **10â€“15% of the exam**, as the same principles and techniques are applied when finding and interpreting solution sets. Mastering linear equations and systems is one of the most effective ways to improve your overall Regents performance.
